## 1 Wavelet-based estimation with multiple sampling rates
##                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      abstract
## 1   We suggest an adaptive sampling rule for obtaining information from noisy\nsignals using wavelet methods. The technique involves increasing the sampling\nrate when relatively high-frequency terms are incorporated into the wavelet\nestimator, and decreasing it when, again using thresholded terms as an\nempirical guide, signal complexity is judged to have decreased. Through\nsampling in this way the algorithm is able to accurately recover relatively\ncomplex signals without increasing the long-run average expense of sampling. It\nachieves this level of performance by exploiting the opportunities for\nnear-real time sampling that are available if one uses a relatively high\nprimary resolution level when constructing the basic wavelet estimator. In the\npractical problems that motivate the work, where signal to noise ratio is\nparticularly high and the long-run average sampling rate may be several hundred\nthousand operations per second, high primary resolution levels are quite\nfeasible.\n

## 2 Attributing a probability to the shape of a probability density

## 2   We discuss properties of two methods for ascribing probabilities to the shape\nof a probability distribution. ## 3 Bump hunting with non-Gaussian kernels
##                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 abstract
## 3   It is well known that the number of modes of a kernel density estimator is\nmonotone nonincreasing in the bandwidth if the kernel is a Gaussian density.\nThere is numerical evidence of nonmonotonicity in the case of some non-Gaussian\nkernels, but little additional information is available. ## 4 Bandwidth choice for nonparametric classification
##                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           abstract
## 4   It is shown that, for kernel-based classification with univariate\ndistributions and two populations, optimal bandwidth choice has a dichotomous\ncharacter. If the two densities cross at just one point, where their curvatures\nhave the same signs, then minimum Bayes risk is achieved using bandwidths which\nare an order of magnitude larger than those which minimize pointwise estimation\nerror. On the other hand, if the curvature signs are different, or if there are\nmultiple crossing points, then bandwidths of conventional size are generally\nappropriate. The range of different modes of behavior is narrower in\nmultivariate settings. There, the optimal size of bandwidth is generally the\nsame as that which is appropriate for pointwise density estimation. These\nproperties motivate empirical rules for bandwidth choice.\n

## 5 Testing for monotone increasing hazard rate
##                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  abstract
## 5   A test of the null hypothesis that a hazard rate is monotone nondecreasing,\nversus the alternative that it is not, is proposed. Both the test statistic and\nthe means of calibrating it are new. Unlike previous approaches, neither is\nbased on the assumption that the null distribution is exponential. Instead,\nempirical information is used to effectively identify and eliminate from\nfurther consideration parts of the line where the hazard rate is clearly\nincreasing; and to confine subsequent attention only to those parts that\nremain. This produces a test with greater apparent power, without the excessive\nconservatism of exponential-based tests. Our approach to calibration borrows\nfrom ideas used in certain tests for unimodality of a density, in that a\nbandwidth is increased until a distribution with the desired properties is\nobtained. However, the test statistic does not involve any smoothing, and is,\nin fact, based directly on an assessment of convexity of the distribution\nfunction, using the conventional empirical distribution. The test is shown to\nhave optimal power properties in difficult cases, where it is called upon to\ndetect a small departure, in the form of a bump, from monotonicity. More\ngeneral theoretical properties of the test and its numerical performance are\nexplored.\n
